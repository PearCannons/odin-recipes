Potato Tacos

Description

These delicious tacos are a staple of my lunchtime cravings. They are very simple to make, technically requiring nothing but instant potatoes, corn tortillas and oil. Obviously there are practically infinite topping options: cheese, salsa, hummus, cilantro, guac, and lots of different sauces.

These tacos also keep for about a week, meaning a busy, hungry individual can pack 3 or 4 of these on a weekend for lunches throughout the week. I recommend keeping toppings in their own separate containers until lunchtime, avoiding shell sog.

Ingredients

  • Instant potatoes, prepared
  • Corn tortillas
  • Vegetable or olive oil
  • (Optional)1-2 avocados, pitted and sliced into 1" widths
  • (Optional)Salsa of your choice
  • (Optional)Cheese (I like mexican style and mozz)

To make

  1. Prepare potatoes according to package directions. 1-2 tsp of water makes a noticeable difference in potato texture, so prep according to preference.
  2. Drop 1-2 Tbsp oil into a large, flat pan over medium heat. Wait for oil to spread across pan evenly.
  3. Lay corn tortillas on pan 1 or 2 at a time for about 20 seconds, until evidently more pliable when lifted.
  4. Drop taco sized amount of potato onto tortilla, fold in half and cook for 2-3 minutes until shell is light or medium brown and stiff.
  5. Repeat with preffered amount of tacos.
